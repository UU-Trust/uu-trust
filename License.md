## Purpose

UU-Trust.org publishes materials documenting the **UU Protocol** and the **Human-Origin Layer**, including conceptual foundations, technical research, and policy guidance.  

The materials describe the architecture of **Human-Origin Proofs (HOPs)**, **Proof-of-Humanity (POH)**, and **Proof-of-Identity (POI)**.  
These mechanisms are immutable, privacy-preserving, and neutral.  
Unauthorized alterations or deployments could result in aggressive tracking, behavioral exploitation, or misuse of the Human-Origin Layer.

## Permitted Uses

UU-Trust.org materials may be used for:

1. **Research & Analysis**  
   - View, inspect, and study the materials for academic research, security analysis, or transparency reviews.

2. **Citation & Discussion**  
   - Reference the concepts and designs in publications, presentations, or teaching materials.

3. **Non-Commercial Experimentation**  
   - Controlled experiments or prototyping, provided they do **not deploy or modify the protected HOP, POH, or POI mechanisms in production or commercial systems**.

## Prohibited Uses

Without explicit written permission from **Uniting Users (UU)**:

- Deploy or replicate core protocol mechanisms outside UU’s stewardship.  
- Integrate materials into systems for surveillance, tracking, or behavioral exploitation.  
- Create derivative infrastructures that violate privacy-first or zero-storage principles.

## Stewardship & Public Good

Uniting Users (UU) is the **creator and steward** of the UU Protocol.  
The Human-Origin Layer and related materials are provided to:

- Ensure transparency and public auditability.  
- Preserve privacy, neutrality, and zero-storage principles.  
- Maintain the integrity of pre-deployment research and ecosystem guidance.  

Stewardship ensures that, until a **self-sustaining UU Protocol stewardship model** is operational, all use aligns with long-term privacy, neutrality, and public-benefit objectives.
